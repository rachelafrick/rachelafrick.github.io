Paper vs. Online

Rachel Africk

November 5, 2020

9:56am

My first exposure to crossword puzzles was probably similar to yours. For me, this was in the Arts section of the New York Times. Crosswords were something done in newspapers, or at the very least on paper. There was the physical action of crossing off clues once I completed them, and the slight panic upon realizing I filled out a section wrong, in pen nonethess. However, this was all part of the game.

That is, until I began doing the puzzles online. I use the NYT Crossword app, which just requires a subscription to the paper. And let me tell you, it's a whole new ballgame. Rather than seeing all the clues at once, you are presented with them one at a time as it highlights the area you are filling in. And, if you get stuck, there is an option to check a letter, word, or even the whole puzzle. The most authentic crossword solving experience? Perhaps not. More fun? For me, yes.
